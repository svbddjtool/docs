---
title: "Rearrange tracks on the board"
description: "Drag track cards up and down to reorder within a phase, or across columns to change phases, while transition badges update in real time."
---

The board is designed for hands-on editing. You can move any track card by dragging it — either within its current phase column to adjust the order, or into a different column to change its phase entirely. Transition badges and the arc chart update as you drag, so you get immediate feedback on every move.

## Reorder within a phase

To change where a track sits within its phase, drag the card up or down inside the same column. The other cards in that column shift out of the way as you move.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Pick up the card">
    Click and hold anywhere on the track card you want to move.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Drag vertically">
    Move the card up or down within the column. A drop indicator shows where the card will land.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Release to place">
    Let go of the card. The transition badges on surrounding cards update immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Move a track to a different phase

To change a track's phase, drag its card horizontally into a different column. You can also move it vertically while dragging across to place it at a specific position within the target column.

<Note>
  A track card snaps to the position closest to where you release it. You can fine-tune the position with a second drag if needed.
</Note>

## How transition badges update

Every card (except the last in the set) shows a transition badge displaying a score and a technique label — for example, "Smooth 92 • Long EQ blend" or "Workable 71 • Breakdown entry or echo out". As you drag a card to a new position, the badges on the card above and below its new landing spot recalculate.

This means you can evaluate a potential move before you commit to it. If the badge score drops sharply when you hover over a position, that is a signal the transition is risky.

<Tip>
  A transition badge with a low score or a "Risky Hop" label does not prevent you from placing the track there — it is information, not a block. Some intentional tension is part of a good set.
</Tip>

## How the arc chart updates

The arc chart in the header reflects the energy curve of your full set. Each time you drop a card in a new position, the chart redraws to show the updated arc. Watch for:

- **Sudden spikes or drops** in the middle of a phase, which often mean a track is misplaced
- **A flat plateau in the Peak phase** — that is usually what you want
- **A smooth descent through Landing** — sharp climbs in the last column cut against the comedown effect

## Reading risky hops while dragging

When a transition badge turns red or shows a very low score as you hover over a target position, you are looking at a risky hop. Common causes:

- A large BPM jump between adjacent tracks (more than ~10 BPM in most contexts)
- A key clash with no harmonic relationship between the two tracks
- An Energy value jump of more than three or four levels in one step

Use the **Track Inspector** to check the Transition Decoder on the card above or below the problem point. It tells you the specific technique and score driving the badge.
