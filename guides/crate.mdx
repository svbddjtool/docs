---
title: "Using the crate for alternate tracks"
description: "Move tracks you like but aren't sure about to the crate, where they stay available as recommendations without committing them to the set."
---

The crate is a holding area for tracks you want to keep available but are not ready to commit to a specific slot on the board. Think of it as your bench — tracks sitting there are not in the set, but they are not gone either. The recommendations panel surfaces crate tracks as "best from crate" suggestions, so good alternates stay visible while you work.

## What the crate is for

A track count of zero in the crate means every imported track is placed on the board — your set is fully committed. As you add tracks to the crate, the board becomes more focused while the bench gives you options to swap in if something does not land.

The crate is a good place for:

- A track that fits the vibe but makes a particular slot feel overcrowded
- A backup that works in two or three different spots — you can read the recommendations panel to see where it scores best
- Tracks you imported speculatively and want to evaluate before placing

## Move a track to the crate

There are two ways to send a track to the crate:

<Steps>
  <Step title="From the Track Inspector">
    Click the track card to open the inspector, then click **Move to Crate**. The card leaves the board immediately.
  </Step>
  <Step title="By dragging">
    Drag the track card off its column and drop it into the crate drop zone. The zone is labeled "Drop tracks here to keep them on the bench."
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Move a track back to the board

To return a crate track to the board, drag it out of the crate and drop it into the column and position you want. Energy Arc places it there and recalculates transition badges for the surrounding cards.

<Note>
  Dropping a track back on the board does not re-run Auto Stage-Fit Set. If you want the track slotted automatically, use **Auto Stage-Fit Set** after placing it, or drag it to the position you prefer manually.
</Note>

## How crate tracks appear in recommendations

The recommendations panel shows a **Best from crate** section that ranks your benched tracks by how well they would fit the position you are currently inspecting or hovering over. This makes it easy to audition alternates without dragging each one in and out manually.

When the crate is empty, the Best from crate section is not shown — there is nothing to recommend.

## When to use the crate

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Phase feels crowded" icon="layer-group">
    If one column has noticeably more tracks than the others, move the weakest fits to the crate instead of deleting them.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Track doesn't fit its slot" icon="shuffle">
    If a track scores badly against its neighbors but you still like it for the set, bench it and check the recommendations panel for a better slot.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Audition before committing" icon="eye">
    Import extra candidates deliberately, then use the crate to park them while you shape the main set. Pull them in when a gap appears.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Swap planning" icon="repeat">
    If you play multiple rooms or back-to-back sets, keep room-specific alternates in the crate so they are easy to pull in with a drag.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>
