---
title: "Reading your set statistics at a glance"
description: "The Quick Readouts panel gives you six live statistics that reveal structural imbalances in your set before they become problems on the night."
---

The **Quick Readouts** panel sits on the board and updates every time you move a track. It gives you six statistics that summarise the health of your set structure: how your opening tracks are distributed, whether your peak has the right weight, how much recovery space you have at the end, how many tracks are still uncommitted, and which specific transitions are your strongest and weakest.

## The six statistics

### Invitation density

**Invitation density** counts the number of tracks currently in the Introduction phase and attaches a short label that tells you whether that count is healthy.

| Label | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Nice spread | The Introduction phase has a well-balanced number of tracks for a warm-up |
| Too thin | Very few tracks in the intro; the opening may feel rushed |
| Overcrowded | Too many tracks committed to the intro; consider moving some to Early Groove or the crate |

Use this stat to avoid front-loading the set with too many slow builds or, conversely, starting the night with a single track and jumping straight into the groove.

### Peak payload

**Peak payload** counts the tracks in the Peak phase and labels the intensity.

| Label | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Healthy danger | A solid number of peak tracks — enough to sustain the climax without burning out the crowd |
| Thin at the top | Too few peak tracks; the climax may feel brief or underpowered |
| Overloaded | Too many tracks at peak energy; the crowd may fatigue before the landing |

A healthy peak has enough tracks to let the energy linger at its highest point, but not so many that the intensity stops feeling special.

### Release space

**Release space** counts the tracks in the Landing phase.

| Label | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Comedown has room | Enough landing tracks to bring the crowd down gracefully |
| Abrupt ending | Very few landing tracks; the set ends before the crowd has fully come down |
| Over-extended | Too many tracks in the landing; the comedown may drag and lose the crowd's attention |

The landing is often underplanned. Check this stat if you have been pulling tracks out of the Landing phase to solve problems elsewhere on the board.

### Crate size

**Crate size** counts the tracks that are in the crate — that is, imported but not committed to any phase.

| Label | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Everything committed | No tracks left in the crate; every imported track has a phase |
| *n* tracks uncommitted | That many tracks are still available as swaps or additions |

A large crate is not a problem — it means you have options. Use the **Smart Recommendations** panel to see which crate tracks fit best where.

### Smoothest hop

**Smoothest hop** names the specific track pair anywhere on the board with the highest transition score, and shows that score. This tells you where the set flows most naturally and gives you confidence that at least one section of the board is working well.

Use this as a reference point: if you can understand *why* that pair scores so highly (matching keys, close BPM, similar energy), you can apply the same logic when building other parts of the sequence.

### Riskiest hop

**Riskiest hop** names the track pair with the lowest transition score, and shows that score. This is where the board is most likely to produce a jarring moment for the crowd.

The riskiest hop is always worth investigating. It is not always worth fixing — sometimes the rough edge is intentional — but you should know it is there.

## Spotting imbalances

Use the six stats together to diagnose structural problems before you export.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="The peak feels too intense for too long">
    Check **Peak payload**. If the label is "Overloaded", move one or two peak tracks into the crate or shift them into the Lift phase. The peak should feel like a destination, not a plateau.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="The crowd might not come down in time">
    Check **Release space**. If the label is "Abrupt ending" or the count is very low, move a track from the crate or from the tail of the Peak phase into the Landing column.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="The opening feels thin or rushed">
    Check **Invitation density**. If it reads "Too thin", look for crate tracks with low energy levels and pull one or two into the Introduction phase.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="One transition keeps causing problems">
    Look at **Riskiest hop** and cross-reference with the **Risky hops** section in the Smart Recommendations panel for a full diagnostic breakdown of that specific pair.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="You are not sure which tracks to commit from the crate">
    Check **Crate size** alongside the Best from Crate suggestions in the Smart Recommendations panel. The suggestions are ranked by fit for each phase, so you can commit the most useful tracks first.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Tip>
  The Quick Readouts panel does not replace the arc chart or the transition score badges — it works alongside them. Use it for a fast structural health check, then use the arc chart and score badges to tune the fine details.
</Tip>
