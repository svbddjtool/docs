---
title: "Edit track tags with the Track Inspector"
description: "Click any track card to open the Track Inspector, where you can override auto-detected tags and read the outgoing transition score."
---

Energy Arc guesses tags for each track based on its metadata, but the algorithm does not always get it right. A track that reads as mid-energy in the data might hit much harder in a room. The Track Inspector lets you override any tag and see exactly how the change affects the outgoing transition.

## Open the inspector

Click any track card on the board. The Track Inspector panel opens, showing the track's current tags and the Transition Decoder for the step out of that track.

## Editable fields

The inspector exposes every tag that Energy Arc uses to score transitions and fit phases.

### BPM and Key

**BPM** and **Key** are the raw values imported from your track list. If the source data is wrong or you want to override what was pasted in, edit them directly here.

### Energy

**Energy** is a 1–10 scale representing how much heat the track brings to the room. A 1 is a near-silent ambient piece; a 10 is full peak-floor material. This value has a direct effect on which phase the track fits and how the arc chart draws.

### Role

**Role** describes the function the track plays in a set:

| Role | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| SETTLE | Brings the energy down and gives the floor a breath |
| SIMMER | Holds a steady level without pushing or dropping |
| NUDGE | Moves the energy up gradually |
| SPIKE | Jumps the energy quickly |
| RELEASE | Provides a cathartic drop or outro moment |

### Vocals

**Vocals** sets the vocal density of the track: `LOW`, `MID`, or `HIGH`. Energy Arc uses this when smoothing transitions — back-to-back HIGH vocal tracks can feel cluttered.

### Drums

**Drums** describes the drum character: `SOFT`, `ROLLING`, or `PUNCHY`. This affects transition scoring when adjacent tracks have clashing drum feels.

### Vibe

**Vibe** is the atmospheric character of the track. Select one:

`ORGANIC` · `MELODIC` · `INDIE` · `DARK` · `RAVEY`

Vibe is used by **Auto Stage-Fit Set** to cluster similar tracks within a phase lane and smooth the feel of transitions.

## Apply your changes

After editing any field, click **Apply Changes** to write the new values back to the track. The transition badges on surrounding cards recalculate immediately.

<Warning>
  Navigating away from the inspector without clicking **Apply Changes** discards any edits you have made in that session.
</Warning>

## Move a track to the crate

Click **Move to Crate** to pull the track off the board and onto the bench. The track stays available and appears in the recommendations panel as a "best from crate" suggestion. See [Using the crate for alternate tracks](/guides/crate) for details.

## Transition Decoder

The bottom of the inspector shows the **Transition Decoder** for the step out of this track — the connection to the next card in the set. It displays:

- **Technique** — a label describing the recommended mixing approach, such as "Long EQ blend", "Phrase-aligned bass swap", or "Breakdown entry or echo out"
- **Score** — a numerical rating (0–100) of how smooth that transition is expected to be
- **Sub-labels** — short diagnostic notes on BPM, key, and texture compatibility

Use the Transition Decoder to understand _why_ a badge is scoring the way it is, and to decide whether changing a tag will help. For example, if the sub-labels show a key clash and you know the track's key was imported incorrectly, fixing the key here and clicking **Apply Changes** will give you an accurate score immediately.

<Tip>
  If you know a track better than the algorithm does — it is darker and punchier than the tags suggest, or it builds slower than the BPM implies — this is the place to say so. Accurate tags lead to better auto-fit results and more meaningful transition scores.
</Tip>
