---
title: "Import tracks into Energy Arc"
description: "Add your track list to Energy Arc by pasting directly from a spreadsheet or CSV, or load the built-in demo set to explore the board right away."
---

Before you can build a set, Energy Arc needs your tracks. You can paste them in from Excel or a CSV file, or load the demo set to try out the board without any prep work.

## Paste from a spreadsheet or CSV

Energy Arc reads tab-separated data, so copying a selection directly from Excel and pasting it into the import field works without any extra formatting.

**Recommended column order:**

| Column | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Track Title | Name of the track |
| Artist | Performing artist |
| Album | Album or release name |
| BPM | Beats per minute |
| Time | Track duration |
| Key | Musical key |

<Steps>
  <Step title="Copy your track data">
    Select the rows in your spreadsheet, including the header row. Copy the selection. Tab-separated values from Excel paste in correctly without modification.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the import dialog">
    Click **Import Tracks** in the Energy Arc toolbar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Paste and confirm">
    Paste your clipboard contents into the text area, then confirm the import. Energy Arc reads each row as one track.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  If your spreadsheet has extra columns beyond the six listed above, that's fine — Energy Arc reads the first six and ignores the rest. Make sure the column order matches.
</Tip>

Once the import finishes, your tracks appear in the track list, ready for you to click **Generate Board**.

## Load the demo set

If you want to explore Energy Arc before committing your own data, click **Load Demo Set**. This populates the board with a pre-built set of tracks so you can try dragging, editing, and generating without any setup.

## Clear the board

<Warning>
  Clicking **Clear** removes all imported tracks and any board layout you have built. This cannot be undone. Save your board first if you want to keep your work.
</Warning>

Use **Clear** to start fresh when you want to import a completely different track list.
